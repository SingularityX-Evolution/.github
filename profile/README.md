## Two Participation Routes: Challenge Bounties and Organization Membership

SingularityX provides two clear participation routes. The first route is to solve public challenge problems and receive challenge bounties after review. The second route is to apply to join the SingularityX organization, receive fixed compensation within agreed responsibilities after formal admission, and receive additional contribution-based compensation through the value assessment mechanism when adopted contributions create verifiable value.

### Our Basic Principles

- **Open tasks, not open control**
- **Open collaboration, without abandoning risk control**
- **Encourage contributions, not black boxes**
- **Allow AI participation, but never let AI make the final decision**

Any area involving fund permissions, live execution, key parameters, core accounts, or sensitive systems must go through strict review, permission isolation, and human verification.

## Open Challenges and Bounty Review

Public challenges publish SingularityX engineering and research problems for reproducible financial research systems. Each challenge is reviewed against its linked challenge page, assigned difficulty level, CNY bounty cap, task period, reproducibility requirements, and acceptance requirements:

```text
Read problem -> Implement artifact -> Prepare evidence package -> Private email submission by default / Public PR optional -> Reproducibility review -> Bounty review result
```

A valid submission should be reproducible. The linked GitHub-rendered challenge page presents the public background and core problem. Detailed review and bounty recognition are handled through the challenge review process.

After review, accepted challenge deliverables may enter the bounty recognition process under the challenge rules. Review considers not only the final result, but also reproducibility, completion against the linked challenge page, marginal value, risk impact, reusability, stability, audit evidence, and submission quality. Challenge results may form a contribution record and may be used as evidence when applying to join the organization.

## Challenge Code Submission

For competitive bounty challenges, SingularityX uses **private submission by default**. Public Pull Requests are accepted only when the contributor intentionally chooses to make the submitted code, report, and implementation details publicly visible.

### Default: private email submission

Unless a challenge statement specifies another private submission channel, contributors should submit the work to the official SingularityX contact email:

```text
join@singularityx.tech
```

Recommended email subject:

```text
[Challenge Submission] <Challenge ID> - <Contributor Name or GitHub Username>
```

A valid private submission should include:

1. Challenge ID;
2. contributor name and GitHub username;
3. contact email;
4. code package, private repository link, or another approved delivery format;
5. commit hash or package SHA256 hash when available;
6. README and reproduction instructions;
7. test results, experiment logs, evaluation report, or generated outputs;
8. dependency and environment information;
9. known limitations;
10. a statement confirming that the contributor owns or has the right to submit the work;
11. a statement confirming that the submission does not include credentials, private market data, private account records, customer information, private trading logs, private positions, private risk rules, non-public strategy material, or private profit logic.

Pasting source code only into the email body is not recommended. A compressed package, private repository link, or structured submission package is preferred.

SingularityX will record the submission time, submitted version, hash or commit reference, review process, reproducibility result, and bounty review outcome.

Once submitted, a submission may not be changed; SingularityX will provide a consolidated response within three calendar days after the task deadline.

### Optional: public Pull Request

Contributors who intend to make their submission publicly visible may fork the repository and submit a Pull Request. Public PR submissions are reviewed under the same technical criteria as private submissions. For competitive bounty challenges, private email submission is recommended to reduce premature disclosure of implementation details and avoid disputes over duplicated submissions.

After review, accepted work may enter the bounty recognition process according to the selected challenge rules. If the contributor applies to join the organization, the accepted result may also be used as capability evidence and contribution evidence.

## Value Assessment and Fair Allocation After Joining the Organization

This section applies to members who have passed review and formally joined SingularityX. Public challenge bounties follow the linked challenge statement and bounty review rules. After formal admission, value assessment focuses on adopted long-term contributions, reusable organizational assets, verification quality, governance discipline, and fair allocation.

### Public scope

This homepage describes the **policy-level framework, algorithmic discipline, governance rules, appeal process, and public version framework**. Detailed calculation settings, operational implementation, and case-level materials are handled through controlled review workflows, formal agreements, and applicable compliance requirements.

| Publicly described | Governed through controlled workflows |
|---|---|
| Assessment objects, evidence requirements, allocation process, rule logic, review discipline, appeal mechanism, public version framework | Calculation settings, implementation details, member-specific records, settlement records, and case materials |

### Methodology: assessment is linear; value is nonlinear

The framework separates assessment scores from real value.

- **Assessment is linear**: comparison, ranking, explanation, budget conservation, and governance execution require stable and reproducible scoring rules.
- **Value is nonlinear**: real value may arise from collaboration, substitution effects, path dependency, timing, reuse, market state, and organizational network effects.
- **Governance connects both layers**: linear assessment creates order, nonlinear attribution approximates true contribution, and ledger constraints convert the result into reviewable allocation decisions.

### Public layered framework

| Layer | Name | Role |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | Intake / Router | Routes tasks, review rights, and delivery paths according to capability, historical quality, workload, and permission status. |
| L1 | Protocol | Defines participation, deliverables, acceptance criteria, evidence requirements, pool rules, and appeal rules before work begins. |
| L2 | Contribution Record | Records tasks, code, research, documents, reviews, quality work, corrections, and reuse as auditable events. |
| L3 | Aggregation & Assessment | Converts heterogeneous evidence into interpretable scores, task points, quality tiers, and verification signals. |
| L4 | Rights Mapping | Maps assessment and attribution results into bonus weights, resource priority, project opportunities, review eligibility, or other agreed incentives. |
| L5 | Settlement & Audit | Produces periodic settlement reports from assessment results, adopted outcomes, ledger facts, appeals, and applicable allocation rules. |

### Dual-ledger structure

The framework separates operating order from economic settlement:

| Ledger | Frequency | Purpose | Properties |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operating Points Ledger | High frequency | Routing, resource priority, project opportunities, review eligibility, and collaboration management. | Fast, simple, interpretable; not equity, tokens, or fixed cash. |
| Economic Settlement Ledger | Low frequency | Additional contribution-based compensation, bonus review, long-term incentive review, or other formal arrangements. | Auditable; tied to adopted outcomes, verification confidence, reuse, governance records, and appeals. |

Core principle: **the operating ledger supports efficiency; the settlement ledger supports fairness.**

## Reward Score: Reward Scoring Illustration

Reward assessment is not based only on returns or hours worked. It focuses on **verified marginal contribution, reusable asset value, quality, risk discipline, and long-term organizational contribution**.

### 1) Evidence gate

No evidence, no allocation. A contribution must first become an identifiable, traceable, and reviewable contribution asset.

Core objects include:

| Object | Description |
|---|---|
| Contribution Asset | A code, research, data, documentation, product, governance, review, or community contribution record. |
| Evidence Event | A task, submission, test result, review, correction, reuse record, or acceptance record linked to the asset. |
| Protocol | The role requirements, acceptance criteria, evidence requirements, pool rules, and appeal rules for the task. |
| Settlement Epoch | The agreed settlement period, such as a month, quarter, or project milestone. |
| Reward Proof | A traceable proof of contribution, assessment, rule version, and allocation result. |

Evidence discipline:

- evidence with missing source, weak linkage, or poor reproducibility may be discounted or flagged;
- self-attested evidence can be recorded, but cannot receive full independent-evidence weight by itself;
- contribution shares must satisfy range and conservation constraints;
- material allocation decisions should be bound to rule versions, assessment versions, review records, and appeal status.

### 2) Linear assessment layer

The assessment layer compresses heterogeneous evidence into comparable, reproducible, and auditable scores. It does not claim to equal true value.

Evaluation dimensions usually include:

| Dimension | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Difficulty | Task difficulty and scarcity of capability. |
| Impact Scope | Impact on a strategy, module, system, team, process, or public good. |
| Reusability | Reuse potential and long-term asset value. |
| Quality | Delivery quality, reproducibility, documentation quality, and maintainability. |
| Risk Discipline | Adjustment for fragility, complexity, permission sensitivity, compliance, and stability. |
| Verification Confidence | Credibility of evidence chains, tests, reviews, audit records, and recalculation basis. |
| Protocol Compliance | Whether the contribution follows task protocol, role requirements, and submission discipline. |

Public rule-level expression:

```text
reward_score
= difficulty_base
× impact_multiplier
× reuse_multiplier
× quality_multiplier
× protocol_compliance_multiplier
× verification_confidence
× risk_discipline_adjustment
```

This expression is used for operating points and governance input. It is not final cash, equity, tokens, or any fixed economic right.

### 3) Pool governance

Allocation is split by protocol so that the last visible submitter is not the only party rewarded.

| Pool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Participant Pool | Direct delivery, research, engineering, testing, documentation, review, and maintenance. |
| Squad Pool | Long-term collaboration, retrospectives, and continued squad-level building. |
| Public Goods Pool | Shared modules, reusable tools, standards, runbooks, audit mechanisms, infrastructure, and shared knowledge assets. |
| Knowledge Asset Pool | Research notes, design documents, ADRs, reviews, retrospectives, tutorials, and related knowledge assets when applicable. |

Pool ratios are determined by task protocols, member agreements, organizational rules, and settlement epochs. Any example ratio is illustrative only and is not a standing commitment.

## Allocation Process

A typical allocation cycle may include:

| Stage | Content |
|---|---|
| Contribution Registration | Record contribution ID, author, version, hash, associated task, and submission time. |
| Evidence Verification | Check source, linkage, reproducibility, acceptance status, and review record. |
| Protocol Check | Confirm task boundaries, role requirements, pool rules, and appeal rules. |
| Linear Assessment | Calculate comparable assessment scores using the agreed public rule logic. |
| Nonlinear Attribution | Evaluate marginal contribution, counterfactual necessity, collaboration effects, and stability constraints. |
| Quality / Reuse / Risk Review | Adjust for quality, reuse, maintainability, permission sensitivity, compliance, and stability. |
| Ledger Verification | Reconcile assessment results with adopted outcomes, review records, corrections, and appeals. |
| Pre-Settlement Report | Produce a reviewable draft result and reasoning summary. |
| Appeal Window | Allow contributors to raise evidence omissions, attribution errors, or rule-application disputes. |
| Human Review | Responsible reviewers or committees review AI suggestions, reports, anomalies, and appeals. |
| Final Settlement | Confirm the allocation result under the applicable rules and agreements. |
| Reward Proof | Generate traceable reward proof and audit records. |

### Nonlinear attribution logic

When multiple people, modules, or knowledge assets jointly create an outcome, the framework uses nonlinear attribution to handle marginal contribution and collaboration effects.

Publicly described logic:

1. **Marginal-Contribution Anchor**: average marginal contribution provides an initial fairness anchor and reduces bias toward title, submission order, or the final visible submitter.
2. **Counterfactual Necessity**: asks whether the result would materially deteriorate without the contribution; shallow, replaceable, or weakly necessary contributions should not receive full allocation.
3. **Quality, Risk, and Compliance Adjustment**: discounts or blocks contributions with weak reproducibility, poor maintainability, rule violations, or unresolved review issues.
4. **Least-Core Stability Constraint**: checks whether the allocation materially underpays important collaboration groups and reduces instability in the settlement result.
5. **Hybrid Settlement**: keeps the marginal-contribution anchor when stability gaps are low and projects toward a more stable allocation when gaps are high.

Public expression:

> **Marginal contribution anchors fairness; Least-Core constrains coalition stability; evidence, protocol, quality, risk discipline, counterfactual necessity, ledger facts, and human review determine final settlement discipline.**

### Settlement conditions

Settlement may be delayed, discounted, or blocked when:

- evidence is missing or cannot be reviewed;
- protocol requirements are incomplete;
- contribution shares do not satisfy conservation constraints;
- appeals remain unresolved;
- material compliance, permission, or governance issues remain open;
- audit chains, rule versions, or assessment reports do not match;
- reviewer conflicts of interest are not disclosed.

### Governance discipline

The allocation system follows three rules:

1. **AI may assist; humans remain accountable.** AI can summarize evidence, suggest preliminary scoring, identify inconsistencies, and draft explanations. Final decisions, appeals, conflict arbitration, rule changes, permission approvals, and governance responsibility remain with humans.
2. **Anti-manipulation is part of the rule system.** Low-quality asset flooding, circular references, exaggerated attribution, undisclosed assumptions, unreproducible results, protocol bypassing, and undisclosed review conflicts may lead to discounting, delayed settlement, permission limits, or organizational review.
3. **Human adjustment must be reviewable.** Adjustments require recorded reasons, conservation checks, conflict-of-interest controls, version records, and an appeal window.

### Public version framework

The following version framework can be publicly described:

| Layer | Public Positioning | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Public Homepage | Public GitHub entry point | Challenge board, organization membership, proof of contribution, value assessment framework, governance discipline, and compliance boundary. |
| Contribution Asset Layer | Contribution record layer | Converts code, research notes, design documents, reviews, runbooks, retrospectives, and community work into contribution assets. |
| Evidence & Assessment Layer | Evidence and scoring layer | Connects contribution assets with evidence events, task protocols, quality review, verification confidence, and operating points. |
| Attribution & Stability Layer | Fair allocation layer | Combines marginal-contribution anchoring, counterfactual necessity, pool governance, and Least-Core stability constraints. |
| Settlement & Appeal Layer | Review and settlement layer | Produces pre-settlement reports, handles appeals, applies human review, and records final settlement decisions. |
| Governance Hardening Layer | Rule quality layer | Adds rule-version tracking, policy checks, conflict-of-interest controls, audit records, and appealable Reward Proof. |

Illustrative public fields:

```text
reward_proof_id

evaluation_round_id
rule_version

evaluation_report_hash
settlement_epoch

```

Reward Proof is not a token, security, equity instrument, payment instrument, or fixed cash value. It is a proof record for the contribution assessment and distribution process.

### AI can participate in evaluation, but cannot make the final decision

| AI Can Do | Humans Must Be Responsible For |
|---|---|
| Evidence extraction and summarization | Final allocation decisions |
| Preliminary scoring suggestions | Appeal handling |
| Code-review suggestions | Conflict arbitration |
| Risk and quality explanations | Rule revision |
| Document summaries | Permission approval |
| Allocation suggestions | Accountability and governance supervision |

> **Principle: AI participates in evaluation; humans are ultimately responsible.**

## Compensation Structure After Joining: Fixed Compensation + Additional Contribution-Based Compensation

After review and formal admission to SingularityX, eligible members may receive fixed compensation and may also enter the additional contribution-based compensation process when adopted contributions generate verifiable value.

### 1. Fixed Compensation

Fixed compensation is used to provide formal members with baseline income within agreed roles, responsibilities, work cycles, and delivery scopes.

### 2. Additional Contribution-Based Compensation

If a member contribution is adopted by the system, passes evaluation, and produces verifiable value, it may enter the **additional contribution-based compensation allocation process**.

Additional contribution-based compensation may correspond to value created through:

- critical system construction;
- high-value research results;
- reusable tools or infrastructure;
- risk-control rules that significantly reduce risk;
- processes or documentation that clearly improve organizational efficiency;
- strategies, data, models, or agent skills that can be reused over the long term;
- contributions that materially affect partnerships, governance, recruiting, and ecosystem development.

> **Note**
>
> All arrangements involving salary, bonuses, profit sharing, equity, options, tokens, points, or other economic interests are subject to formal agreements, applicable law, tax rules, compliance requirements, risk-control rules, and organizational approval results.
